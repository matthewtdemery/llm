# Red Flags and Guardrails

## Red flags in the call

- “Let’s just build the full app.”
- “The UI is the app.”
- “All streaming and all theaters will be easy.”
- “We do not need user tests because the idea is obvious.”
- “The developer will figure out the data sources later.”
- “Marketing will be easy once it is in the app store.”
- “Ticket affiliate revenue will quickly pay back development.”

## Calm response

```text
I agree the vision is bigger. I’m trying to make sure the first paid step produces evidence, not just screens. The risky part is not whether we can make it look good — it is whether users care enough and whether the data/routing layer can be made reliable at a sane cost.
```

## Non-negotiable guardrails

1. Validate the wedge before full build.
2. Ask for data-source plan before vendor quote is trusted.
3. Separate prototype cost from production cost.
4. Separate development budget from API/data/marketing budget.
5. Avoid promising universal showtime/ticketing coverage in version 1.
6. Keep version 1 movies-only; do not accidentally become another TV-streaming guide.
7. Measure actual user behavior, not just verbal enthusiasm.
